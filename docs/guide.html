Overview

Vipu is designed to answer one simple question: "Do I have enough money to cover next month's expenses?"

Unlike apps that track every transaction, Vipu focuses on the big picture. You set up your recurring income and expenses once, then just update your account balances periodically. The app does the math to show your financial position.

Vipu has two main features:

  • Weekly Budget - Track if you can cover upcoming expenses
  • Net Worth - Track your total wealth over time with monthly snapshots

Weekly Budget

The "One Month Ahead" Philosophy

Vipu assumes you want to always have enough money to cover next month's expenses. This means:

  • Expenses stay listed even after you've paid them this month
  • There are no due dates - every expense is "due" continuously
  • Your goal is to maintain a positive "net position" at all times
Example

Your rent is 1,200 EUR/month. Even after paying January's rent, the expense stays listed because you'll need to pay February's rent next month. If your balance drops below 1,200 EUR, Vipu shows you're not prepared for the upcoming rent.

Understanding the Summary Cards

The budget page shows three summary cards:

  1. Current Position = Total account balances - Monthly expenses
    Can you cover next month right now?
  2. After This Month = Current Position + Net income
    Where will you be after this month's salary?
  3. With Savings Goals = After This Month - Savings targets
    What's left after setting aside savings?

Accounts vs Credit Cards

Vipu separates regular accounts from credit cards:

  • Accounts (checking, savings) - Money you have
  • Credit Cards - Money you owe (shown as negative)

Both are included in your total balance. A checking account with 3,000 EUR and a credit card with -500 EUR means your total balance is 2,500 EUR.

Tip: Vipu assumes you pay credit cards in full each month. If you carry a balance, the negative amount correctly reduces your available funds.

Income and Deductions

Income items can be marked as taxed or untaxed. For taxed income, Vipu calculates net income using your tax percentage (set in settings).

Deductions are items that reduce your paycheck before it hits your bank account (like lunch benefits or pension contributions). These are separate from expenses because they never appear in your bank - they're handled by your employer.

Expenses vs Savings Goals

  • Monthly Expenses - Recurring costs (rent, utilities, subscriptions)
  • Savings Goals - One-time targets you're working toward (emergency fund, vacation, new computer)

Both reduce your available funds, but savings goals are shown separately in the third summary card.

Net Worth Tracking

Monthly Snapshots

Net worth tracking works differently from the budget. Instead of real-time updates, you create a snapshot once per month recording all your assets and liabilities.

This approach is intentional:

  • Less maintenance than tracking every account daily
  • Focuses on long-term trends, not daily fluctuations
  • Works well for assets that don't change often (property, investments)

Groups and Categories

Net worth is organized into groups and categories:

  • Groups are visual containers (Cash, Investments, Property, Loans, etc.)
  • Categories are the actual line items within groups (Checking Account, Savings Account, Stock Portfolio, etc.)

You can customize both. The default groups are just suggestions - create whatever makes sense for your situation.

Personal vs Company Wealth

Each category can be marked as personal or company-owned. This is useful for:

  • Finnish entrepreneurs with a holding company (Oy)
  • Anyone who wants to track business assets separately
  • Understanding what you can actually access personally

Charts and Trends

The net worth page shows:

  • Area chart - Your net worth over time
  • Pie chart - Current allocation by group
  • Summary cards - Current total, monthly change, year-to-date change

Financial Goals

Goals let you set targets and track progress. Each goal has:

  • A target amount
  • An optional target date
  • A tracking method (total net worth or specific category)
Example Goals
  • "Reach 100,000 EUR net worth by December 2025" - tracks total net worth
  • "Build 10,000 EUR emergency fund" - tracks a specific savings category
  • "Pay off car loan" - tracks a loan category (goal is 0 EUR)

Goals appear on the net worth chart as horizontal lines, making it easy to see your progress visually.

Recommended Workflows

Weekly Routine (5 minutes)

  1. Open the Budget page
  2. Update each account balance with the current amount
  3. Check your "Current Position" - is it positive?
  4. If you paid off a credit card, update that balance too
Tip: Pick a consistent day (like Sunday evening) to update balances. The habit matters more than the exact numbers.

Monthly Routine (15 minutes)

  1. At the end of each month, go to Net Worth
  2. Click "Add Snapshot" and select the month
  3. Enter current values for each category
  4. Review the charts to see your progress
  5. Optionally: update any income/expense changes for next month

Getting Set Up (30-60 minutes, one time)

  1. Add your bank accounts and credit cards
  2. Set up your income sources (salary, side income)
  3. Add your monthly expenses (rent, utilities, subscriptions)
  4. Set your tax percentage in settings
  5. Create net worth categories that match your accounts
  6. Add your first net worth snapshot
  7. Optionally: set up financial goals

Getting Started

When you first open Vipu, the database is empty. You have two options:

  1. Load example data - Use the "Seed Data" buttons to load sample data and explore the app
  2. Start fresh - Add your own accounts, income, and expenses manually
Note: The seed data is for demonstration only. Make sure to delete it and enter your real data before using Vipu seriously.
